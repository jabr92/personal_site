Personal

I usually introduce myself with an explanation: "John Arin. Both parts are my first name." My academic interests include computers/technology and the physical sciences, especially chemistry. I learned to program on a TI-84 calculator because I was frequently bored in high school math classes, and repeatedly solving Pythagorean theorem problems became tedious. In addition to gaining and applying technical knowledge, I find satisfaction in exploring wilderness areas, tending to my garden, and preparing meals for those I love.

Experience

My first job was with the Community Service Officer Program of the UC Berkeley Police Department. I helped keep campus and the surrounding community safe, starting with my boots on the ground and eventually serving as Administrative Program Coordinator for the final two and a half of my more than five years with the program.

During my time at UC Berkeley I also worked in the Thermal Hydraulics Laboratory of the Department of Nuclear Engineering. There I helped design and build a working model to validate the emergency shutdown system for a next-generation nuclear reactor.

My most recent position was at Hurricane Electric, which operates a global internet backbone network and hosts servers in Silicon Valley. My main responsibility was diagnosing and resolving customers' technical issues, but I spent the majority of my time programming.

To see some of the programming projects I have worked on, check out my cool stuff page.

Education

I spent my senior year of high school on the campus of my local community college, where I took courses in calculus, physics, and computer science. My experiences there solidified my love of the physical sciences, but also piqued my interest in programming.

I decided to study chemistry at UC Berkeley since it's easy to own a computer and download software but hard to own a lab and obtain reagents. In addition to physical science coursework, I also studied Russian language and the art of meditation.
