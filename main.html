Personal

I usually introduce myself with an explanation: "John Arin. Both parts are my first name." I was born and raised in Redding, CA then moved to the San Francisco Bay Area, where I still live, for college. My academic interests include computers/technology and the physical sciences, especially chemistry. In addition to gaining and applying technical knowledge, I find satisfaction in exploring wilderness areas and preparing meals for those I love.

Experience

My first job was with the Community Service Officer Program of UCPD Berkeley. I helped ensure the safety of the UC Berkeley campus community starting with my boots on the ground and working up to serving as Administrative Program Coordinator for the final two and a half of my more than five years with the program.

During my time at UC Berkeley I also worked in the Thermal Hydraulics Laboratory of the Department of Nuclear Engineering. The goal of my project was to validate an emergency shutdown system for a next-generation nuclear reactor.

Currently I work at Hurricane Electric, which operates a global internet backbone network and hosts servers in Silicon Valley. My main responsibility is diagnosing and resolving customers' technical issues, but I spend the majority of my time programming. Every day I learn new things about how computers and the internet work.

To see some of the prgramming projects I have worked on, check out my cool stuff page.

Education

I spent my senior year of high school on the campus of my local community college, where I took courses in calculus, physics, and computer science. My experiences there solidified my love of the physical sciences, but also piqued my interest in computers.

I decided to study chemistry at UC Berkeley since it's easy to own a computer and purchase software, but hard to own a lab and purchase reagents. I completed 84% of courses for a B.S. in chemistry, but for now have decided to pursue a career in the Bay Area's prolific tech industry rather than complete my formal studies.
